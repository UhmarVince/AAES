Understanding Soil Liquefaction Potential in Philippine Infrastructure Projects

Engr. Uhmar Vince Aceron
Principal Structural Engineer | April 18, 2026

The Philippines is situated in a seismically active region. When addressing structural integrity, it is important to consider the ground conditions. Soil liquefaction is one such phenomenon that warrants attention, especially in areas with loose, saturated sandy soils subjected to strong ground shaking.

Understanding where and when liquefaction might occur is a foundational step in project planning. Simple site investigations, including Standard Penetration Tests, can provide initial indicators of soil susceptibility. We look at soil types, water table depth, and seismic design parameters to make informed assessments. For a general overview of our work, please see our main page.

Addressing liquefaction risk involves careful evaluation during the design phase. Mitigation strategies can range from ground improvement techniques to adjusting foundation types. The specific approach depends on the project's scale, site conditions, and economic viability. Our goal is to ensure the stability and safety of structures built in challenging environments.
